Kline Cocktail Compendium

Champagne Flute
This glass is the ideal vessel for a cocktail that is made with sparkling wine. Its tall, narrow shape allows visualization of the rising bubbles. Don't overstir these cocktails, as this will flatten the drink!

Martini Glass
This iconic conical glass is classically used for Martinis and related clear (and generally stirred, not shaken!) cocktails. The wider versions are tricky to sip from without spillage, especially after the first one goes down!

Coupe
This wide, flat bowl-shaped glass presents many cocktails with style. Less apt to spill than the Martini glass, it is a common vessel for shaken cocktails, usually with citrus, that are served up

Highball Glass
This glass is appropriate for tall drinks that served on the rocks. Often it is served with a straw. Sometimes a cocktail umbrella!

Nick & Nora Glass
This tulip-shaped cocktail glass was named after Nick and Nora Charles, a fictional couple created by Dashiell Hammett in his novel The Thin Man. A private detective and his wealthy wife, they embody the hard-drinking 1930s post-flapper culture. The glasses are pleasing to hold and are appropriate for any cocktail that is served straight up.

Double Rocks or Old-Fashioned Glass
These classic glasses are usually heavy in the hand, as fits the seriousness of their contents: Manhattans, Old-Fashioneds, and similar cocktails that are usually clear and mixed by stirring.
