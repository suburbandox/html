The Martini

The martini is a drink with a lot of baggage. James Bond erroneously orders it shaken, not stirred; shaking this spirit-forward concoction "bruises" the liquor and over-dilutes the drink. Classic martinis may be made with either vodka or gin as the spirit base, or both, as in the case of the Bond-inspired Vesper; the drink is reduced in ferocity by the addition of dry vermouth, the amount of which depends on the taste of the imbiber. The less vermouth, the drier the Martini. Noel Coward is reputed to have said that the perfect martini should be made by filling a glass with gin, then waving it in the general direction of Italy. Note to the wise: the abominations that may be found in a "martini-bar" that include chocolate and other sweeteners are not martinis, if indeed they deserve the name of cocktail!
