The Old Fashioned

The Old-Fashioned cocktail is at its heart a core spirit (traditionally whiskey), a sweetener (typically sugar-based, but can alternately be fruit-oriented), and a bittering agent. There are many Old-Fashioned variants. It traditionally has been "built" in the serving glass, meaning that the ingredients are added directly to the glass, then ice, and it is stirred until well chilled.

Simple syrup: this classic sweetener is basically sugar dissolved in water. It may be 1:1 sugar and water or for a more concentrated sweetness 2:1 (meaning twice the volume of sugar as water). For rapidity of dissolving, the syrup is often heated up, but it can sit at room temperature with frequent shaking. The syrup keeps well (though not indefinitely) in the refrigerator. Simple syrups are often flavored with an infusion of herbs, fruits, or other seasonings.
